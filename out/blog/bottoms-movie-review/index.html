Bottoms (2023): Subverting Teen Comedy Conventions

September 24, 2023

An analysis of how Bottoms reimagines the teen comedy genre through sharp writing and unconventional storytelling.

Emma Seligman's "Bottoms" marks a significant shift in LGBTQ+ storytelling within mainstream cinema. Rather than focusing on coming-out narratives, the film presents its queer characters as fully realized individuals whose identities are established facts rather than plot points. This approach, brought to life by stellar performances from Ayo Edibiri (The Bear) and Rachel Sennott (Shiva Baby), demonstrates how queer narratives can transcend traditional frameworks while maintaining broad appeal.

The film's greatest strength lies in its masterful balance of tone. It approaches serious themes through an absurdist lens, particularly in its treatment of violence. Physical confrontations are portrayed with cartoonish exaggeration, serving as metaphors for empowerment and self-discovery while maintaining the film's comedic momentum. This stylistic choice allows "Bottoms" to tackle weighty subjects without sacrificing its entertainment value.

The third act presents an interesting case study in genre conventions. While the film largely succeeds in subverting teen comedy tropes, it ultimately follows familiar patterns in its conflict resolution, particularly regarding PJ's character development. This adherence to conventional structure, while potentially limiting the film's subversive potential, creates an accessible framework for its more experimental elements.

"Bottoms" succeeds through its commitment to authentic representation and sharp dialogue, even when working within genre constraints. While the late introduction of antagonists feels somewhat forced, and the tonal balance occasionally wavers, these are minor critiques of an otherwise significant contribution to contemporary cinema. The film's ability to prioritize humor while maintaining genuine LGBTQ+ representation signals a welcome evolution in mainstream comedy.
