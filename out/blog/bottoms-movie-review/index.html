Bottoms (2023) - A Fresh Take on Teen Comedy

September 24, 2023

Emma Seligman's feature film "Bottoms" presents a refreshing departure from traditional coming-out narratives. Instead, we focusing on characters who are confidently established in their identities. Starring Ayo Edibiri (The Bear) and Rachel Sennott (Shiva Baby), the film demonstrates how queer storytelling can transcend conventional narrative frameworks while maintaining broad audience appeal.

The film's distinctive tone balances absurdist comedy with social commentary, particularly evident in its treatment of violence as both empowerment tool and comedic device. While physical confrontations are portrayed with cartoonish levity, they serve as metaphors for broader themes of self-discovery and empowerment. This stylistic choice enables the film to explore serious subjects while maintaining its comedic core.

However, the film's adherence to certain genre conventions, particularly in its third act, somewhat undermines its otherwise innovative approach. The inevitable conflict and reconciliation between the main characters feels formulaic, especially given PJ's established character arc. While this narrative choice aligns with the film's heightened reality, it represents a missed opportunity to fully commit to its subversive potential.

Despite these structural compromises, the film succeeds through strong performances and sharp dialogue. While the tonal balance occasionally wavers and the late introduction of antagonists feels contrived, "Bottoms" remains a significant contribution to the evolution of queer cinema in mainstream comedy. Its willingness to prioritize humor over drama while maintaining authentic LGBTQ+ representation marks an important shift in the genre's landscape.
